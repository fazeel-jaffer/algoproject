About Us

PRmePRed is a powerful prediction tool based on Support Vector Machine (SVM) that accurately predicts arginine methylation sites in proteins. In eukaryotes, protein arginine methylation plays a vital role in various important biological processes. PRMTs (Protein arginine methyltransferases) are responsible for methylating a diverse range of substrates, including histones, transcription factors, and several proteins involved in RNA processing, transport, and translation. By utilizing sequence and structure-based properties, this SVM-based classifier has been developed to effectively identify potential arginine methylation sites in proteins. The performance of PRmePRed is quite promising, achieving an accuracy of 84.10% in 10-fold cross-validation. The tool exhibits high sensitivity (82.38%) and specificity (83.77%), indicating its ability to correctly identify true positive and true negative instances. The Matthew's correlation coefficient of 0.7 further validates its reliability in making precise predictions. With its remarkable accuracy and robustness, PRmePRed can significantly aid researchers and biologists in understanding the functional implications of arginine methylation in various biological pathways and processes. By highlighting potential methylation sites, this tool contributes to the exploration of novel regulatory mechanisms and facilitates the discovery of key protein targets involved in cellular activities. As such, PRmePRed stands as a valuable asset in advancing our understanding of protein arginine methylation and its impact on cellular functions.
